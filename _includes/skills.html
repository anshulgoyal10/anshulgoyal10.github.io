Data Science & Machine Learning

Since 2018, I have been the main (usually sole) developer of machine learning models built at RoadBotics, including the current deep learning model used to grade pavement damage for RoadWay, the company’s flagship product.

I have also thoroughly examined RoadBotics’ terabytes of proprietary image+geospatial data to understand and compensate for its biases during model training.

Software Engineering

To enable my data science work at RoadBotics, I wrote many internal libraries and built several scalable software pipelines on AWS to manipulate terabytes of data flowing through the company. I used popular Python libraries and frameworks for this work, including NumPy, Pandas, SciKit-Learn, Keras+TensorFlow, and Jupyter Notebooks.

Before my current position at RoadBotics, I worked on software development in several other companies including Apple and Amazon. See the full list in my Work Experience section.

Technical Research

Beyond building machine learning models, I did extensive research and prototyping using another class of computer vision algorithms, known as SLAM, to unlock more sophisticated analysis of urban environment from our collection of visual data. SLAM is a class of computer vision algorithms that can reconstruct a 3D model of a real-world scene from 2D images of the original scene. Much of this research work is captured in a series of company blog posts written by me and Miguel Dickson, my old boss.
