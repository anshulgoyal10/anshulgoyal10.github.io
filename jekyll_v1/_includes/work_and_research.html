Work & Research

Data Scientist

RoadBotics
February 2018 — Present

My work at RoadBotics is in deep learning and computer vision research for automating visual damage assessment of city assets, mainly roads:

  • Developed deep learning models used to assess road surface damage, including the current model used in our RoadWay product
  • Created a scalable software pipline on AWS to convert a video taken by a smartphone while driving down a city street into 3D point-cloud model of the street and surrounding city block using SLAM
  • Used the SLAM pipeline as a basis for prototyping new product features:
    • 3D models of city blocks
    • GPS localization of city assets (e.g. street signs) with high precision
    • Detection of environment changes using multiple models of the same scene from different times
    • Automatic measurement of assets (e.g. sidewalk with for ADA compliance)
  • Inventor on international patent application PCT/US2021/032820
  • Principal investigator on two Small Business Innovation Research grants:
    • USDA National Institude of Food and Agriculture grant proposal 2022-00851: Few-Shot Learning for Rapid Urban Forest Inventorying (applied November 2021 and awaiting response)
    • NSF SBIR Phase 1 grant proposal 8205053: Leveraging non-optimal data for automated sidewalks assessment and inventory (applied February 2022 and awaiting response)
  • Gave multiple tech talks and wrote several blog posts on RoadBotic's AI and research efforts

 

Applied Machine Learning Research

UPMC
May 2018 — August 2019

In my research for Dr. Arman Kilic, a cardiac surgeon at University of Pittsburgh Medical Center, I applied machine learning to hospital pre-surgery patient data for cardic surgery patients to see if we could improve upon current risk-prediction models for post-surgery patient outcome. The following publications were based on this work:

  • Kilic, A., Goyal, A., Miller, J. K., Gleason, T. G., & Dubrawksi, A. (2021). Performance of a machine learning algorithm in predicting outcomes of aortic valve replacement. The Annals of Thoracic Surgery, 111(2), 503-510. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.athoracsur.2020.05.107
  • Kilic, A., Goyal, A., Miller, J. K., Gjekmarkaj, E., Tam, W. L., Gleason, T.G., Sultan, I. and Dubrawksi, A. (2020). Predictive utility of a machine learning algorithm in estimating mortality risk in cardiac surgery. The Annals of Thoracic Surgery, 109(6), 1811-1819. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.athoracsur.2019.09.049

Also mentored first-year Masters students using machine learning to predict post-operative patient outcomes.
